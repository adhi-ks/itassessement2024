Rock

A genre characterized by a strong rhythm, simple chord structures, and electric instruments.

Period: 1950s - Present
Instruments:
Electric Guitar Bass Guitar Drums Vocals
Characteristics: High energy, powerful vocals, distorted guitars

Jazz

A genre that originated in the early 20th century featuring improvisation and complex harmonies.

Period: 1900s - Present
Instruments:
Saxophone Trumpet Piano Double Bass Drums
Characteristics: Improvisation, swing rhythm, complex harmony

Electronic

Music that primarily uses electronic instruments and digital technology.

Period: 1940s - Present
Instruments:
Synthesizer Drum Machine Computer Sampler
Characteristics: Synthesized sounds, programmed beats, digital effects

Classical

Traditional Western art music with roots in liturgical and secular music.

Period: 1600s - Present
Instruments:
Violin Piano Cello Flute French Horn
Characteristics: Complex structures, orchestral arrangements, formal composition

Pop

Popular music aimed at a general audience with catchy melodies and hooks.

Period: 1950s - Present
Instruments:
Vocals Guitar Piano Drums Synthesizer
Characteristics: Catchy melodies, mainstream appeal, commercial production

Hip Hop

A culture and art form featuring rhythmic speech over strong beats.

Period: 1970s - Present
Instruments:
Turntables Microphone Drum Machine Sampler
Characteristics: Rhythmic speech, sampling, strong beats, urban culture
